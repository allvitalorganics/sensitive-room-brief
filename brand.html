1 · What you get for your fee

For a chapter sponsorship fee of $3,000 to $4,000, your brand commissions a wave of organic, creator-made content and plugs into a vetted creator pipeline you can keep working for a full year. This covers the Chapter 01 workshop on Thursday, June 25, 2026, 7PM at Haus of K, Brooklyn. A chapter seats up to 50 creators, and every accepted creator must publish 3 organic pieces. That is up to 150 organic pieces produced in one chapter, allocated fairly across the sponsoring brands.

The night runs in two parts at the same venue: the 7PM K-beauty workshop and networking roll straight into a 10PM World Cup watch party (USA match, kickoff TBC), giving the sponsor extra dwell time with the cohort and more content out of one evening.

The value case is the volume of organic content produced, plus the coverage, intro slot, shortlist, and pipeline. Payment: 100% of the sponsorship fee is due within 7 days of the workshop date (a single payment, no installments).

5 · The organic content the brand receives

Each accepted creator must produce 3 organic pieces as a mandatory condition of joining the workshop, regardless of which brand. Sensitive Room runs the allocation so coverage is fair to every brand:

  • 1 sponsoring brand: the creator produces all 3 pieces for that brand.
  • 2 sponsoring brands: Sensitive Room assigns a 2 + 1 split per creator, rotated across the cohort so each brand gets balanced coverage.
  • 3 sponsoring brands: the creator produces 1 piece for each brand.
  • 4 or more sponsoring brands: Sensitive Room selects 3 brands per creator so coverage stays balanced and fair to every brand.

When there is more than one brand, Sensitive Room (not the creator) selects which brands a creator covers, to keep allocation fair across brands. As a planning figure, a single sponsor can expect roughly 150 pieces at 1 brand, 75 at 2 brands, 50 at 3 brands, and about 38 at 4 brands, scaled to actual cohort size.

Per-piece spec

  • Each piece is at least one in-feed short-form video (Instagram Reel or TikTok is acceptable per piece).
  • Each piece must tag the Brand handle and @sensitiveones.agency.
  • Each piece must carry a clear FTC material-connection disclosure (#ad, #sponsored, or the platform Paid-partnership label), per Section 7.

Timing & tracking

  • The first piece is published within 14 days of the workshop; all 3 are published within 30 days of the workshop.
  • Creators post live links in the Discord #content-submissions channel; Sensitive Room logs each to the tracker so delivery is auditable.
  • After organic pieces are submitted, Sensitive Room reviews and selects creators to link with brands for paid branded content and longer-term relationships.
6 · Brand follow-up obligation (the trust mechanism)

This is the obligation the Brand takes on in exchange for free organic content. The Brand commits to pursue at least 2 paid or branded deals from the cohort within 60 days of the workshop, sourced and booked through Sensitive Room.

Why this exists Creators give the Brand 3 pieces of unpaid organic content up front. In return, the Brand owes a real, good-faith path to paid work. The 2-deal minimum within 60 days is what turns "free content" into a fair exchange: the creator gets a genuine pipeline, not just exposure. A sponsorship is only worth running if both sides are accountable, so the follow-up is a binding commitment, not a courtesy.

"Good-faith paid deal offers" means real, commercially serious offers for paid or branded work to cohort creators, extended and booked through Sensitive Room within the 60-day window. The Brand is not required to contract with any specific creator, but it is required to actually extend offers meeting this minimum.

7 · Content usage license & FTC responsibility

License grant

  • Creators retain ownership of their organic content. The Brand receives a license, not an assignment.
  • The Brand receives a non-exclusive, royalty-free license to repost and reshare each creator's 3 organic pieces on Brand-owned and operated channels (Brand social profiles, stories, website, and email or owned channels), with creator credit retained, for 6 months from each piece's publication date.
  • This base license covers organic use only. It does not include any paid amplification, boosting, whitelisting or allowlisting (for example Meta Partnership Ads), or TikTok Spark Ads.
  • Any paid amplification or whitelisting requires separate written, per-creator consent and is not covered by this base license. If it is not written, it is not granted. Per current market norms, the brand should expect to negotiate and pay separately for any paid or whitelisted use, and the creator must supply the platform access or authorization for it.

Market context (2025 to 2026): organic reposting on brand-owned channels is commonly bundled at no extra fee within a defined 6 to 12-month window, while paid amplification and whitelisting are priced and consented separately. On license expiry, the Brand must stop creating new posts and stop all boosting or whitelisting; already-published organic reposts may remain live but may not be boosted, edited, or re-licensed.

FTC compliance responsibility

Both the Brand and the creator share legal responsibility for disclosure under the FTC's Guides Concerning the Use of Endorsements and Testimonials in Advertising, 16 CFR Part 255 (revised, effective July 26, 2023). The Brand agrees that:

  • There is no truly "organic" carve-out: because creators receive value from the Program and the sponsoring brands, a material connection exists and must be clearly and conspicuously disclosed in every endorsing post.
  • A disclosure must be unavoidable and placed in the endorsement itself. It is not adequate if a viewer must click "more," tap a link, hover, or scroll to see it; in video it must appear in the video, not only the description.
  • A platform's built-in "Paid partnership" label alone is not a safe disclosure; creators add their own (for example #ad) on top.
  • When the Brand boosts, whitelists, or otherwise amplifies a creator's content, the Brand assumes primary FTC and platform advertising-compliance responsibility for the amplified version.
  • The Brand will not direct or pressure any creator to omit, bury, or weaken a required disclosure, or to post claims about the Brand's products the creator does not honestly believe or that lack Brand-provided substantiation.

FTC duties apply to both organic and paid posts regardless of contract wording; this Agreement allocates responsibility but cannot waive these legal duties. For beauty and skincare claims specifically, the Brand is responsible for the truthfulness and substantiation of any product claims its sponsorship invites.

8 · Deal roll-up & commission
  • Deal window. Any deal between the Brand and a cohort creator within 12 months of the workshop is transacted through Sensitive Room. This covers the initial deal and any repeat, renewal, or expansion business with the same cohort creator inside the window.
  • Commission. Sensitive Room earns a commission of 25% of the branded-deal gross value on each such deal.
  • Single window. The Brand agrees to route cohort-creator deals through Sensitive Room rather than around it for the duration of the window, so attribution and the trust model stay intact.
In plain terms Sensitive Room introduced you to these creators and stands behind the relationship. For 12 months, deals you do with them run through Sensitive Room at a 25% commission on the deal's gross value.
9 · Consequence if the brand fails follow-up

If the Brand fails to extend good-faith paid deal offers meeting the 2-deal minimum within its 60-day window (Section 6), then:

  • Affected creators may revoke the Brand's usage license to their organic content. At the creator's choice, the content may be removed, and the Brand must stop using and reposting that creator's pieces.
  • The Brand's access to the cohort shortlist and to future Sensitive Room chapters may be suspended or declined, at the Program Operators' discretion.
  • The Brand acknowledges the reputational consequence: failing the creators who gave free organic content is treated as a serious breach of the Program's trust model and may be disclosed to the cohort.

This linkage is what makes the free organic content fair: the creator gives content now on the understanding that a real paid pipeline follows. If the pipeline does not materialize from the Brand, the creator gets the content back.

10 · Brand intro slot logistics
  • Length: a live 2-minute brand-introduction slot delivered to the full cohort during the in-person workshop.
  • Format: the Brand may present in person or send a representative; a short supporting visual (a few slides or a brief clip) is welcome. The slot is an introduction to the brand, its products, and what it is looking for in creators, not a hard-sell pitch.
  • Materials: the Brand delivers any slides, clip, or product talking points to Sensitive Room at least 3 business days before the workshop. Sensitive Room confirms running order and on-site logistics.
  • Conduct: the slot follows the Program's tone and time limit. Sensitive Room may keep time and may decline content that is unsafe, dishonest, or off-brief.
11 · Brand marks license & conduct

The Brand grants Sensitive Room (SOA and Nurilounge) and the Program a limited, non-exclusive, royalty-free, revocable license to use the Brand's name, logo, and marks solely to promote and run the Chapter (the landing page, cohort materials, event coverage, and recap content) during the Term, subject to the Brand's reasonable brand guidelines. The Brand may review use of its marks; approval will not be unreasonably withheld and is deemed given if the Brand does not object within 2 business days of a request.

  • The Brand delivers usable logo and brand assets to Sensitive Room at least 5 business days before the workshop.
  • The Brand will conduct itself professionally toward creators and cohort members and will not engage in conduct materially damaging to the Program.
  • This marks license ends on expiry or termination of this Agreement, except that already-published Chapter recap and coverage content may remain live with the Brand's name credited.
12 · Representations, indemnity & limitation

Representations

The Brand represents that it has the authority to enter this Agreement, that it owns or controls the marks it licenses here, that its products and any product claims it asks creators to feature are lawful and substantiated, and that it will comply with applicable advertising and consumer-protection law, including the FTC Guides and New York General Business Law.

Indemnity

The Brand will indemnify and hold harmless SOA, Nurilounge, the Program, and their personnel from third-party claims, losses, and penalties arising from: (a) the Brand's products or product claims; (b) the Brand's own use, amplification, or whitelisting of creator content; (c) the Brand's breach of this Agreement; or (d) the Brand's failure to meet its own FTC or advertising-compliance duties. Sensitive Room will indemnify the Brand for claims arising from Sensitive Room's gross negligence or willful misconduct in operating the Program.

Limitation of liability

Except for indemnity obligations and breaches of the usage or marks license, neither party is liable to the other for indirect, incidental, or consequential damages. Each party's total aggregate liability under this Agreement is capped at the sponsorship fee paid for the Chapter. The Program is offered without a guarantee of any specific sales, reach, or deal outcome.

13 · Term, governing law & signatures

Term. This Agreement runs from the date signed through the later of (a) the end of the 12-month deal window in Section 8, and (b) the expiry of the last content usage license in Section 7. Sections that by their nature survive (usage, indemnity, limitation, FTC responsibility, commission on deals already booked) survive termination or expiry.

Termination. Either party may terminate for the other's material, uncured breach on 10 days' written notice. Non-payment of the sponsorship fee or non-delivery of brand assets by the deadline lets Sensitive Room withhold the affected benefits or terminate for cause; the Brand remains liable for fees accrued, and Sensitive Room retains amounts paid for benefits already delivered.

Governing law. This Agreement is governed by the laws of the State of New York, without regard to conflict-of-laws rules. The parties submit to the courts located in New York for any dispute.
