What you are agreeing to · plain language

  • You will make one organic video for each sponsoring brand assigned to you, capped at 3 videos total: 1 brand means 1 video, 2 brands means 2 videos, 3 brands means 3 videos, and if 4 to 10 brands sponsor the chapter, Sensitive Room picks 3 brands for you and you make 1 video for each. SOA and Nurilounge act as Co-Operators of Sensitive Room. Where this Agreement grants rights or obligations to Sensitive Room, those rights and obligations run to SOA and Nurilounge jointly as the operators of the Program, and to the Sponsoring Brand(s) where expressly stated. Sensitive Room is the single window through which every brand-to-creator deal in the Program is sourced, handled, and booked.

Section 3

As a condition of attending, you agree to produce and publish one organic video for each Sponsoring Brand assigned to you, capped at 3 videos total. The count tracks the number of brands you are assigned: one brand means one video, two brands means two videos, three brands means three videos. This requirement is mandatory; it is the consideration you provide in exchange for your Workshop seat and the benefits of the Program.

How many videos you owe, and for which brands, depends on the number of Sponsoring Brands in your chapter. When there is more than one Sponsoring Brand, Sensitive Room, not the Creator, selects which brands you cover, and balances assignments across the cohort so coverage stays fair for every brand.

Section 4

Content Specs & FTC Disclosure Obligation

4.1 Per-video specification

Each video you make must meet the following minimum spec:

  • At least one in-feed short-form video (Instagram Reel or TikTok) per assigned brand. Higher-effort formats are welcome but not required.
  • Each video must tag the assigned brand's handle and tag @sensitiveones.agency.
  • Each video must carry a clear FTC material-connection disclosure, as set out in 4.2.
  • Content reflects your honest opinion and actual experience. You will not make claims about a Sponsoring Brand's products that you do not believe to be true or that lack substantiation the Sponsoring Brand provides to you.

4.2 FTC material-connection disclosure

You acknowledge that your participation in the Program creates a “material connection” to the Sponsoring Brand(s) and to Sensitive Room under the FTC's Guides Concerning the Use of Endorsements and Testimonials in Advertising, 16 CFR Part 255 (revised effective July 26, 2023). A material connection exists because you receive value tied to the Sponsoring Brand or the Program, which includes your Workshop seat and access, any gifted product, and any present or anticipated future paid or business relationship through Sensitive Room. You agree to clearly and conspicuously disclose this material connection in every post, story, reel, video, livestream, or other content that references, depicts, tags, or endorses a Sponsoring Brand or its products.

Clear and conspicuous standard. Each disclosure must be unavoidable and placed within the endorsement content itself. You will use an unambiguous term such as #ad, #sponsored, Advertisement, or [Brand] Partner / [Brand] Ambassador, placed at or near the start of the endorsement so viewers see it before scrolling past. You will not rely on vague terms such as “thank you,” “#sp,” “#partner,” “#collab,” or “#gifted” alone, or a brand hashtag alone. The disclosure may not appear only on your profile or About page, only at the end of a caption, or only behind a “more,” “see more,” or expandable link, and a viewer must not have to click, tap, hover, or scroll to see it.

Format-specific placement. For video content, the disclosure must appear in the video itself, as on-screen text and/or audibly, and not only in the description or caption. For image-based or ephemeral content (Instagram Stories, Reels, TikTok, Snapchat), you will superimpose the disclosure on the image or video and keep it visible long enough for an ordinary viewer to notice, read, and understand it.

Platform tools are not enough on their own. Using a platform's built-in “Paid partnership” or “Includes paid promotion” label does not by itself satisfy your disclosure obligation. You must add your own clear-and-conspicuous disclosure (for example, #ad) in the visible body of the content in addition to any platform label. You and Sensitive Room acknowledge that, under FTC guidance, ultimate responsibility for disclosure rests with the creator and the brand, not the platform.

No “organic” carve-out. Although these videos are called “organic” because they are your own creative work on your own channels, they are not unsponsored. No content promoting a Sponsoring Brand may be presented as unsolicited, independent, or unpaid when a material connection exists. The duty to disclose applies regardless of whether the content is framed as a personal recommendation, day-in-the-life, event recap, or unscripted impression.

Reviews and testimonials. You will not post reviews or testimonials that are fake, AI-fabricated, or for products you have not actually used, consistent with 16 CFR Part 255 and the FTC Rule on the Use of Consumer Reviews and Testimonials, 16 CFR Part 465.

Liability is shared Under FTC guidance, both the Sponsoring Brand and Sensitive Room (as intermediary), as well as you, can be on the hook for non-disclosure. That is why this obligation is written into the Agreement. Sensitive Room and Sponsoring Brands may review your content for compliance and may ask you to promptly correct, add a compliant disclosure to, or remove any non-compliant content. This obligation survives termination of this Agreement.
Section 5

Timeline & Tracking

  • First video: published within 14 days of the Workshop.
  • All videos: published within 30 days of the Workshop.
  • Submission: you post the live links in the Sensitive Room Discord #content-submissions channel as each video goes live. Sensitive Room logs each link to the program tracker.

These windows exist so Sensitive Room can confirm delivery, give Sponsoring Brands an accurate coverage count, and decide which creators to link with brands for paid work. Deals are non-exclusive and not guaranteed; cohort creators are set as priority reach-outs for the Sponsoring Brands. After your organic videos are submitted, Sensitive Room reviews the cohort and selects creators to connect with Sponsoring Brands for paid branded content and longer-term relationships.

Section 6

Filming, Photography & Likeness Release

The Workshop is photographed and filmed. As a condition of attending, you grant the release in this Section. New York's right of privacy and publicity is statutory, under New York Civil Rights Law Sections 50 and 51, and requires your prior written consent before your name, portrait, picture, likeness, or voice is used for advertising or trade. This Section is that written consent.

6.1 Grant of likeness rights

You grant to Sensitive Room, SOA, Nurilounge, the Sponsoring Brand(s), and each of their affiliates, successors, assigns, and licensees (together, the “Released Parties”) the right to record, film, photograph, and capture your name, portrait, picture, likeness, image, voice, performance, statements, and biographical information (together, your “Likeness”) at and in connection with the Workshop.

6.2 Consent under New York Civil Rights Law Sections 50 and 51

You acknowledge that this Section is your prior written consent, within the meaning of New York Civil Rights Law Sections 50 and 51, to the use of your name, portrait, picture, likeness, and voice for advertising purposes and for purposes of trade, and you waive any claim under those Sections arising from the uses authorized here.

6.3 Scope of media and permitted uses

The grant covers all media and formats now known or later developed, including digital, social, broadcast, streaming, print, out-of-home, and internet, in whole or in part, edited or unedited, and in combination with other content, text, or imagery. The Released Parties may use your Likeness for advertising, marketing, promotional, editorial, commercial, trade, and internal purposes relating to the Workshop, Sensitive Room, the Co-Operators, and the Sponsoring Brands and their products and services.

6.4 Territory and duration

This grant is worldwide. The event-coverage license, meaning the footage and photographs captured at the Workshop, is perpetual and survives termination of this Agreement. Your consent under this Section is irrevocable to the maximum extent permitted by law. For clarity, the perpetual license in this Section covers event coverage only; the license to your organic videos is the time-limited license in Section 7, not this Section.

6.5 Consideration

You grant these rights for good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which you acknowledge, including your participation in the Workshop and the benefits provided under this Agreement.

6.6 No right of approval; waiver

You waive any right to inspect or approve the finished materials or the uses to which they may be applied, and you release the Released Parties from any claim of defamation, invasion of privacy, right of publicity, or violation of New York Civil Rights Law Sections 50 and 51 arising from any use authorized by this Agreement.

6.7 AI and digital-replica use

This Agreement does not authorize the creation or use of any digital replica, or any AI-generated or AI-enhanced representation, of your likeness or voice. Routine editing such as color correction, cropping, captioning, and minor retouching of real footage is permitted and is not a digital replica. Any synthetic or AI-generated reproduction of your likeness or voice requires your separate, specific written consent stating the scope and purpose, the duration, and the compensation, consistent with current New York standards for AI-likeness consent.

6.8 Minor participants

You represent that you are at least 18 years old. If you are under 18, this Agreement and the grant of Likeness rights in this Section must be executed by your parent or legal guardian, who represents that they have authority to grant these rights on your behalf. The parent or guardian acknowledges that New York courts have generally treated a valid, unrestricted parental or guardian consent as binding on the minor, although this area of law is fact-specific and should be confirmed with qualified counsel before relying on it. A separate parent or guardian signature block is provided at the end of this Agreement.

Section 7

Content Ownership & Brand License

7.1 You own your content

You retain all copyright, moral rights, and intellectual-property rights in your organic videos (the “Content”). No rights are transferred or assigned except the limited license expressly granted below.

7.2 Organic license to the Sponsoring Brand

You grant each Sponsoring Brand you cover a non-exclusive, royalty-free license to repost, display, and reshare your Content on that brand's own and operated organic channels, including its social profiles, website, email, and in-store or owned screens, with your credit and tag retained, for 6 months from the publication date of each video. This license covers organic use only.

7.3 Paid amplification needs separate written consent

The organic license in 7.2 does not include any paid advertising, boosting, whitelisting or allowlisting (such as Meta Partnership Ads), or TikTok Spark Ads, and does not include running ad spend from or attributed to your handle. Any such paid amplification requires your separate, specific written consent per creator, on its own terms and fee, and is conditioned on you providing any necessary platform access or authorization. If it is not written, it is not granted. The Sponsoring Brand assumes primary responsibility for FTC and platform advertising-disclosure compliance on any amplified version it runs.

7.4 On expiry

When the 6-month organic license for a video expires, the Sponsoring Brand will stop creating new posts, ads, or derivatives using that video. Already-published organic reposts may remain live but may not be boosted, edited, or re-licensed.

Section 8

Removal / Revocation Right

This is the trust mechanism that makes your organic content fair. Each Sponsoring Brand commits to pursue at least 2 paid or branded deals from the cohort within 60 days of the Workshop, sourced and booked through Sensitive Room.

If a Sponsoring Brand assigned to you fails to extend good-faith paid-deal offers meeting that minimum within its 60-day window, you may revoke that brand's license to your Content. On revocation, that brand must cease all use of your Content and, at your written request, you may remove the Content from your channels. Revocation applies only to the brand that missed its follow-up minimum and does not affect any other brand you covered, the event-coverage license in Section 6, or Sensitive Room's role as the deal window.

You may also require removal of any video on an objective triggering event affecting the other party, such as illegal conduct, fraud, or widely reported conduct materially harmful to your reputation. Where a cure is possible, the responsible party has 5 days to cure before removal is required.

Section 9

Deal Window & Commission

Deals are non-exclusive and not guaranteed. As a cohort creator, you are set as a priority reach-out for the Sponsoring Brands, but no brand is obligated to extend any particular deal. Any deal that does result between a Sponsoring Brand and a cohort creator within 12 months of the Workshop is transacted through Sensitive Room. Sensitive Room is the single operating window for all brand-to-creator deals in the Program; this is what removes the gray zone and keeps sourcing, handling, and booking in one place. Sensitive Room's commission is 25% of branded-deal gross value. The terms of any individual paid deal are set in a separate deal agreement and are not fixed by this Agreement.

Section 10

Community Conduct & Professionalism

The Sensitive Room Discord is where the cohort coordinates and submits work. You agree to:

  • keep communication respectful and professional with other creators, Sensitive Room staff, the Co-Operators, and Sponsoring Brand representatives;
  • not harass, threaten, defame, or discriminate against any participant;
  • not share confidential program information, including the identity of any Sponsoring Brand that has not yet been publicly revealed, outside the cohort;
  • not spam, solicit unrelated business, or post unlawful content in program channels; and
  • post your live submission links in #content-submissions promptly as required by Section 5.

Sensitive Room may remove from program channels any content or participant that violates this Section, and serious or repeated violations are grounds for removal from the roster under Section 11.

Section 11

Non-Completion & No-Show

These consequences are proportionate, not punitive. They do not impose money damages for failing to complete the deliverable. If you do not attend after accepting, or you do not publish your assigned videos within the windows in Section 5, or you materially breach the conduct rules in Section 10, Sensitive Room may:

  • remove you from the current chapter's roster;
  • treat you as ineligible for future Sensitive Room chapters; and
  • decline to connect you with Sponsoring Brands for paid work from that cohort.

Sensitive Room will not seek monetary penalties or liquidated damages from you for non-completion of the organic deliverable. If you delivered some but not all of your assigned videos in good faith, Sensitive Room may, at its discretion, work with you on a short extension before treating the deliverable as incomplete.

Section 12

Creator Representations

You represent and warrant that:

  • your Content is your original work, or you hold all rights necessary to create and publish it, including rights to any music, footage, images, or third-party material you include;
  • your Content and your posting of it do not infringe any third party's intellectual-property, privacy, or publicity rights;
  • you are at least 18 years old, or, if not, your parent or guardian has signed this Agreement on your behalf;
  • you will disclose your material connection in compliance with the FTC obligations in Section 4; and
  • you have authority to enter into this Agreement and to grant the rights it contains.
Section 13

Indemnity & Limitation of Liability

13.1 Your indemnity. You will indemnify and hold harmless Sensitive Room, the Co-Operators, and the Sponsoring Brand(s) from third-party claims, losses, and reasonable expenses arising from (a) your breach of this Agreement, (b) your failure to make a required FTC disclosure on your Content, or (c) your Content infringing a third party's rights.

13.2 Their indemnity. Sensitive Room, the Co-Operators, and a Sponsoring Brand will indemnify and hold you harmless from third-party claims, losses, and reasonable expenses arising from (a) their breach of this Agreement, (b) their use of your Likeness or Content outside the scope you granted, or (c) FTC or platform-disclosure failures on any paid amplification they run under a separate consent.

13.3 Limitation. To the extent permitted by law, neither party is liable to the other for indirect, incidental, consequential, special, or punitive damages. Except for the indemnity obligations above and a party's willful misconduct, each party's total liability under this Agreement is limited to the value of the consideration exchanged in connection with the chapter. Nothing in this Section limits the statutory rights or remedies available under New York Civil Rights Law Sections 50 and 51 or the FTC rules referenced above.

Section 14

Term, Governing Law, Entire Agreement, Severability

14.1 Term. This Agreement starts when you sign it and continues for the chapter, the deliverable and tracking windows, the 6-month organic license per video, the 12-month deal window, and the perpetual event-coverage license, each as set out above. Provisions that by their nature should survive, including the likeness release, FTC obligations, indemnity, and limitation of liability, survive termination.

14.2 Governing law. This Agreement is governed by the laws of the State of New York, including New York Civil Rights Law Sections 50 and 51, without regard to conflict-of-laws principles. The parties consent to the exclusive jurisdiction of the state and federal courts located in New York for any dispute arising under this Agreement.

14.3 Entire agreement. This Agreement is the entire understanding between you and Sensitive Room about the Workshop and your organic deliverable, and it supersedes any prior discussions on that subject. Any paid deal between you and a Sponsoring Brand is governed by its own separate written agreement.

14.4 Amendment and assignment. Any change to this Agreement must be in writing and signed by both parties. You may not assign this Agreement; Sensitive Room may assign it to a Co-Operator or successor operating the Program.

14.5 Severability. If any provision is held unenforceable, the rest of the Agreement stays in effect, and the unenforceable provision is modified to the minimum extent needed to make it enforceable while preserving its intent.
