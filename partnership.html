Sensitive Ones Agency and Nurilounge propose to co-run Sensitive Room, a creator-into-K-beauty program hosted at Haus of K (Hana House) in Brooklyn. This MOU sets out how the two companies share the work, share the money, and route every brand-to-creator deal through one clean window so there is no gray zone.

Deal at a glance

The four points that matter

  1. Two revenue buckets. Bucket 1 = brand sponsorship fees for each chapter. Bucket 2 = commission on branded creator deals booked through Sensitive Room.
  2. Bucket 1 splits 50:50 (SOA : Nurilounge). Sponsorship fees are shared evenly after any agreed shared event costs (F&B, production) come off the top. The Haus of K venue is Nurilounge's recognized in-kind contribution.
  3. Bucket 2: of Sensitive Room's 25% deal commission, SOA 15% and Nurilounge 10% of deal gross (a 60:40 split of the commission). Sensitive Ones is the single deal touchpoint and carries deal-making, management, and aftercare; Nurilounge sources creators and brands and runs the venue.
  4. One window. Both companies may source and handle creators and brands, but every resulting deal is booked through Sensitive Room. That single window is what removes the gray zone.

Section 2

Program overview & venue

Each chapter of Sensitive Room runs as a sponsored workshop plus a tracked content-and-deal pipeline:

  • Chapter 01. [Event date: TBD] at Haus of K.
  • Cohort. Up to 50 creators per chapter, with a pilot floor of 20 to 30.
  • Sponsorship. One or more brands sponsor each chapter and fund it. Chapter 01's sponsor stays publicly "revealing soon" until the agreed reveal moment.
  • Creator obligation. Each accepted creator makes one organic video per sponsoring brand assigned to them, capped at 3 videos total (1 brand = 1 video, 2 = 2, 3 = 3, 4 to 10 = Sensitive Room picks 3 brands, one video each), as a condition of acceptance, tracked through the Program's submission flow. When there is more than one brand, Sensitive Room assigns which brands a creator covers so coverage stays fair across brands.

Venue. The Program is hosted at Haus of K (Hana House), 345 Adams St, Brooklyn, NY 11201. Haus of K is provided through Nurilounge and runs through November 2026. Workshops are photographed and filmed for Program marketing under the name and likeness terms carried in the creator agreement.

Bucket 2 · Branded-deal commission

Sensitive Room earns a commission of 25% of the gross value of each branded deal booked through it (Section 6). That commission is then split:

PartyBucket 2 share of the 25% commissionEffective share of deal gross
SOA60%15% of deal gross
Nurilounge40%10% of deal gross

A 25% Program commission on a creator-marketing deal sits squarely at the 2026 market anchor for an emerging-creator program; agencies commonly run 15% to 30%. The split of that commission between the two companies is the negotiation lever, not the commission rate itself.

Open item to decide together The two buckets split differently on purpose, by who does the heavy lifting in each. Sponsorship (Bucket 1) splits 50:50 because Sensitive Ones hosts (Daesung) and organizes the event while Nurilounge sources the sponsoring brands and provides the venue: even contribution, even split. The deal commission (Bucket 2) splits SOA 15% / Nurilounge 10% of deal gross (a 60:40 split of the 25%) because Sensitive Ones is the single deal touchpoint and carries deal-making, management, and aftercare end to end, while Nurilounge's contribution is the upfront sourcing. Nurilounge already rents Haus of K and earns its return through this revenue share plus the venue's recognized in-kind value; shared direct event costs (watch-party F&B, production) come off the top of Bucket 1 before the split so no party absorbs them alone. Roles are set in Section 3.
Section 5

Single-window roll-up

This is the mechanic that answers the concern Aram raised about creator sales living in a gray zone.

  1. Open sourcing. Both SOA and Nurilounge may source, handle, and reach out to creators and to brands. Neither party is boxed out of its own relationships, and Daesung connects on behalf of both.
  2. One booking window. Every deal between a sponsoring or participating brand and a cohort creator is booked through Sensitive Room, regardless of which party sourced it. No side deals are booked outside the window.
  3. Deal window. Any deal between a brand and a cohort creator signed within 12 months of that creator's workshop is transacted through Sensitive Room, including repeat and renewal business with the same brand. A simple Deal Register, maintained jointly, records each creator-brand pairing and the workshop date so attribution is never argued after the fact.
  4. Why it works. Because the window is the only booking path, the Program (and therefore both parties) shares fairly in every deal the room produces. There is no incentive to route a creator around the partnership, and no ambiguity about whose deal it was.
Plain version Source freely. Book in one place. Both companies share every deal that comes out of the room, for 12 months per creator. That is the whole gray-zone fix.
Section 6

Commission collection & payout mechanics

One party is the contracting and collecting party for the Program, so the money has a single clean path and the other party is never chasing a customer for its share.

  • Contracting / collecting party. SOA is the sole contracting and collecting party. SOA signs the brand and creator agreements in its own name, issues all invoices, and receives all Program payments (sponsorship fees and branded-deal commission) into a designated Program account.
  • Holding in trust. SOA holds Nurilounge's share of Program revenue in trust and remits it per the cadence below. Sponsorship fees and commission are tracked in separate ledgers so each bucket's split is applied correctly.
  • Net Program Revenue. Each bucket is split on revenue actually received, less only: (i) refunds and chargebacks, (ii) documented third-party payment-processing fees, and (iii) agreed discounts or credits. No other deductions are taken without written consent. There is no open-ended "other costs" line.
  • Reporting. Within 10 days after each calendar month, SOA delivers Nurilounge a statement showing, per bucket: gross received, itemized deductions, net revenue, the split applied, and the amount owed.
  • Payout cadence. SOA remits Nurilounge's share within 15 days of month-end. The parties reconcile monthly.
  • Transparency & audit. On 10 business days' notice, no more than twice per year, Nurilounge may review the relevant Program records. Any underpayment exceeding 5% is paid promptly with the review cost borne by SOA.

Section 8

IP & branding

  • Sensitive Room name. The "Sensitive Room" name and program marks are used jointly for the Program. Each party may use them only to operate and promote the Program, per any brand guidelines the parties agree. Goodwill in the name is held for the Program.
  • Haus of K marks. "Haus of K" and Hana House venue marks belong to Nurilounge and are licensed to the Program for Program use only, for the term of this MOU.
  • Chapter naming. Chapters are named in the agreed nesting: Haus of K › Sensitive Room › Vol I · Chapter 01. Neither party rebrands the Program or a chapter without the other's written agreement.
  • Each party's own marks. SOA marks and Nurilounge marks remain each party's own property. Nothing here transfers ownership of either party's underlying brand.
  • Creator and brand content. Creators keep ownership of their content; brands receive the usage license set out in Exhibit A. This MOU does not move that ownership.
Section 9

Term, exclusivity & termination

  • Term. This MOU runs chapter by chapter. It begins on signature and continues through the Haus of K run, which currently extends through November 2026, renewing by mutual written agreement for further chapters.
  • Exclusivity. For the term, the parties run the Sensitive Room program exclusively together: neither party launches a directly competing creator-into-K-beauty workshop program at Haus of K, or under the Sensitive Room name, without the other. Each party's separate, non-Program business stays its own. Brand isolation across the parties' other brands is respected.
  • Termination for convenience. Either party may end this MOU at the close of a chapter on 30 days' written notice, so an in-flight chapter is never cut off mid-run.
  • Termination for cause. Either party may terminate on 15 days' written notice if the other materially breaches and does not cure within that period (immediately for non-payment of a remittance due or a reputational breach materially damaging the Program).
  • Active deals on termination. Termination does not cancel the economics of deals already in the Deal Register. For each cohort creator's 12-month deal window that is already open at termination, deals that close in that window remain booked through Sensitive Room and revenue is split per Section 4 as received. To keep the tail clean and time-limited, Bucket 2 commission on those post-termination closings is paid at 100% of the in-term split for months 1 to 6 after termination and 50% for months 7 to 12, and 0% thereafter. The collecting and reporting duties in Section 6 continue until the last tail payment is made.
Section 10

Confidentiality

Each party will keep the other's non-public information confidential: deal terms, sponsor identities held pre-reveal, creator data, pricing, the Deal Register, and the financial figures in this MOU. Confidential information is used only to run the Program and is not shared outside each party's team without consent, except where disclosure is legally required. This duty survives termination for 2 years. Public-facing Program communications stay high-level (paid partnerships and the workshop) and do not disclose revenue splits, commission rates, sponsor identities before reveal, or internal margins.

Section 11

Governing law & signatures

This MOU is governed by the laws of the State of New York, without regard to its conflict-of-laws rules. The parties will try in good faith to resolve any dispute between themselves before any formal proceeding.
