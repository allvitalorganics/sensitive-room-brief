2 · Content obligation & allocation engine

One video per sponsoring brand, capped at 3

Every accepted creator makes one organic video per sponsoring brand, capped at 3 total: 1 brand means 1 video, 2 brands means 2 videos, 3 brands means 3 videos. This is a mandatory condition of acceptance into the workshop. When there is more than one brand, Sensitive Room (not the creator) selects which brands a creator covers, balanced so every paying brand gets fair coverage.

Per-video spec

  • At least one in-feed short-form video per assigned brand (Instagram Reel or TikTok is acceptable).
  • Must tag the brand handle and @sensitiveones.agency.
  • Must carry a clear FTC material-connection disclosure: #ad or #sponsored, or the platform paid-partnership label plus the creator's own disclosure (see Section 5 for why the label alone is not enough).
  • Timeline: first video within 14 days, all videos within 30 days of the workshop.

Allocation engine: worked examples

Sponsoring brandsAllocation per creatorHow Sensitive Room balances it

Worked example · the headline number50 creators × 3 brands → 50 videos per brand. With three sponsors, each creator makes one video per brand, so the brand receives one video from each of the 50 creators: 50 organic videos. Total cohort output is 150 videos (50 × 3). With more or fewer sponsors, expect roughly 30 to 50 videos per brand depending on the final sponsor count. That is the volume math a sponsor is buying into.

Why Sensitive Room picks the brands when there is more than one: if creators self-selected, the most attractive brand would get over-covered and a quieter brand would get starved. Centralizing the assignment is what lets us promise every paying sponsor balanced coverage.

3 · Tracking system

One row per creator, up to three videos per row

Everything is trackable because every promise has a column. Each row holds up to three video slots (P1 to P3); a creator fills one slot per assigned brand, so a single-sponsor cohort uses only P1, a two-brand cohort uses P1 and P2, and a three-brand cohort uses all three. The master tracker lives in a shared sheet (Google Sheets or Notion database), owned by the Sensitive Room window. #content-submissions in Discord is the intake; the sheet is the source of truth. The verification step is manual: when a link arrives, the Sensitive Room operator opens it, confirms the spec and FTC disclosure, then logs it.

5 · Submission workflow

Discord in, sheet of record out

Creator publishes a video, then drops the live link in #content-submissions with the assigned brand name (e.g. "ClearDea video").
Sensitive Room logs the link to the matching row and brand-video column in the tracker, stamping the publish date.
Verify spec. Operator opens the link: in-feed short-form video, tags brand handle + @sensitiveones.agency.
Verify FTC. Disclosure must be inside the post, unavoidable, and use an acceptable term (#ad / #sponsored / "[Brand] Partner"). A platform paid-partnership label alone does not pass; the creator must add their own disclosure. If FTC fails, operator flags the creator to fix before counting it (Section 10).
Flip status to live and set FTC = yes, on-time = yes/no against the +14 / +30 windows.
Notify the brand once a creator's assigned videos are complete and verified, so the brand can begin its 6-month organic repost license and shortlist the creator for paid follow-up.
Why the FTC step is strictUnder the FTC Endorsement Guides (16 CFR Part 255, revision effective July 26, 2023), there is no "organic" carve-out once a creator received something of value: a free workshop seat, gifted access, or a present-or-future paid pipeline is a material connection that must be clearly and conspicuously disclosed. Liability runs to the brand and the event organizer, not only the creator. A built-in platform label "might not be an adequate disclosure," so we require the creator to add their own. This is why the disclosure obligation sits in the creator agreement and gets verified on every single video.
6 · Brand deal pipeline & follow-up enforcement

The free content is fair because the brand commits back

The organic videos are not a giveaway. They are the front half of a trade: the brand gets a high volume of UGC (roughly 30 to 50 videos depending on the final sponsor count), and in return the brand commits to actually pursue deals from the cohort. That commitment is what makes the free content fair to the creators. Deals are non-exclusive and not guaranteed: cohort creators are set as priority reach-outs for the sponsoring brands, and any deal that results is booked through Sensitive Room.

  • The minimum. Each sponsoring brand commits to pursue at least 2 paid/branded deals from the cohort within 60 days of the workshop, sourced and booked through Sensitive Room.
  • The deal window. Any deal between a sponsoring brand and a cohort creator within 12 months of the workshop runs through Sensitive Room. This covers the first deal and all repeat, renewal, and expansion business with the same brand.
  • If a brand ghosts. If a sponsoring brand fails to extend good-faith offers meeting the minimum inside its 60-day window, the affected creators may revoke that brand's usage license to their organic content, and that content may be removed at the creator's choice. This revocation right is the linkage that keeps the trade honest.
Tracker linkageThe "Brand follow-up" column is how we enforce this. On day 60, any brand with fewer than 2 offers logged is in breach, and Sensitive Room notifies the affected creators of their revocation right. We track follow-up per brand, not just per creator.
7 · Commission & payout flow

One window collects, two buckets split

Sensitive Room is the single contracting and collecting window. It books the deal, invoices the brand, collects the cash, takes commission, and remits the partner share.

BucketWhat it isSplit (of deal gross)
Bucket 1Sponsorship fees: the brand chapter fees, structured and shared by Nurilounge.50 : 50 (SOA : Nurilounge)
Bucket 2Branded-deal commission: of Sensitive Room's 25% deal commission on cohort deals booked through Sensitive Room.SOA 15% / Nurilounge 10% of deal gross
Confidentiality wallThe 15 / 10 commission split (and any commission percentage) is partner-only: it may appear in this internal Ops doc and the Nurilounge MOU, never on creator-facing, brand-facing, or public surfaces. Brand pricing is structured by Nurilounge; do not publish our own per-brand fee on any brand or public surface.

Commission mechanics

  • Rate: Sensitive Room takes 25% of branded-deal gross value. This is the market-standard single-program anchor for an emerging-creator program (the live band is 15 to 30%; 25% sits squarely in it).
  • Collection: brand pays Sensitive Room. Sensitive Room takes the 25%, then remits the creator's share, then splits its commission SOA 15% / Nurilounge 10% of deal gross.
  • Cadence: reconcile monthly. Send a statement showing gross, deductions, net, and amounts owed before each payout. Remit partner share within 15 days of cleared funds.
8 · Value-of-sponsorship model

The value case is video volume

Brand pricing is structured and shared by Nurilounge, so the value case we frame is video volume, not cost-per-piece. Each sponsoring brand receives one video from every creator assigned to it. With up to 50 creators, expect roughly 30 to 50 videos for the brand depending on the final sponsor count:

SponsorsVideos per brandHow it lands
1 brandup to 50One video from each creator goes to the single sponsor.
2 brandsup to 50 eachEvery creator covers both brands, so each brand still gets one video per creator.
3 brandsup to 50 eachEvery creator covers all three, one video per brand.
4 to 10 brandsroughly 30 to 50 eachSensitive Room picks 3 brands per creator, balanced so coverage stays fair across the cohort.

And the sponsorship buys more than the videos. On top of the content, each sponsor receives:

  • Event coverage and a reusable photo library.
  • A 2-minute brand-introduction slot in the workshop.
  • A vetted creator shortlist.
  • A 12-month deal pipeline booked through Sensitive Room.
How to pitch itLead with the video-volume line for the actual sponsor count (one video per assigned creator, roughly 30 to 50 per brand), then stack the four extras. Pricing is structured by Nurilounge; do not quote our own per-brand fee on brand-facing or public surfaces. Once the Chapter 01 case study exists, Nurilounge can introduce sponsor tiers and category exclusivity.

10 · Risk & edge cases

What goes wrong, and the handling

CaseHandling
Creator no-show (attends, then never posts)Tracker flags the missed +14 / +30 windows. Sensitive Room follows up once, then once more. Non-delivery is a breach of the acceptance condition: the creator forfeits paid-shortlist consideration and is not invited to the next chapter. Their event-footage release still stands.
Brand ghosts on follow-up (under 2 offers by day 60)Brand is in breach of the follow-up minimum. Sensitive Room notifies affected creators of their right to revoke that brand's usage license; content may be removed at the creator's choice. Brand is not offered a renewal chapter until cured.
Participant under 18The name/likeness release and event-footage license require a parent or guardian to sign. Flag at acceptance; do not film or publish a minor's content under the standard adult release. Counsel should confirm child-directed and COPPA considerations before any minor is accepted.
Content-removal requestCreator retains ownership and may request removal of their own videos. Brand's repost license is 6 months from publication and is the only thing the brand may rely on; a valid revocation (brand breach) or a minor-related issue ends the license early. Log the request and the date the brand was notified to take down reposts.
Dispute (attribution, who sourced a brand, deal credit)The tracker and the single-window booking rule are the tiebreaker: if it was not booked through Sensitive Room, it is not a program deal. Sponsorship and partnership terms are governed by the State of New York. Escalate unresolved splits to the Alex<>Aram principals.
FTC non-disclosure by a creatorVerification catches it before the video counts. Operator sets FTC = no, does not count the video, and tells the creator to add a compliant in-post disclosure (acceptable terms only; platform label alone does not cure). Repeated failure is a material breach of the creator agreement and removes the creator from paid consideration. This protects the brand and the organizer, who share liability under the FTC Guides.
