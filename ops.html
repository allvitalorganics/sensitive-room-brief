2 · Content obligation & allocation engine

The always-3-pieces rule

Every accepted creator must produce 3 organic content pieces for the sponsoring brand(s). This is a mandatory condition of acceptance into the workshop, regardless of which brand a creator covers. The total is always three. The allocation engine decides who covers whom so that every paying brand gets fair, balanced coverage, and Sensitive Room (not the creator) selects the brands whenever there is more than one sponsor.

Per-piece spec

  • At least one in-feed short-form video per piece (Instagram Reel or TikTok is acceptable).
  • Must tag the brand handle and @sensitiveroom.hausofk.
  • Must carry a clear FTC material-connection disclosure: #ad or #sponsored, or the platform paid-partnership label plus the creator's own disclosure (see Section 5 for why the label alone is not enough).

Allocation engine: worked examples

Sponsoring brandsAllocation per creatorHow Sensitive Room balances it
1 brand3 pieces for that one brand.No choice needed. Every creator covers the single sponsor.
2 brands2 + 1 split (2 pieces for one brand, 1 for the other).Sensitive Room rotates which brand gets the "2" across the cohort, so over 50 creators each brand lands roughly the same total coverage.
3 brands1 piece each.Clean even split. Each brand gets one piece from every creator.
4+ brandsSensitive Room selects 3 of the brands for each creator (still 3 pieces total).Assignment is rotated so coverage is balanced and fair to every brand across the cohort. No creator covers all four; the engine spreads the fourth brand evenly.
Worked example · the headline number50 creators × 3 brands → ~50 pieces per brand. With three sponsors, each creator makes one piece per brand, so the brand receives one piece from each of the 50 creators: 50 organic pieces. Total cohort output is 150 pieces (50 × 3). That is the volume math a sponsor is buying into.

Why Sensitive Room picks the brands when there is more than one: if creators self-selected, the most attractive brand would get over-covered and a quieter brand would get starved. Centralizing the assignment is what lets us promise every paying sponsor balanced coverage.

5 · Submission workflow

Discord in, sheet of record out

Creator publishes a piece, then drops the live link in #content-submissions with the brand name and piece number (e.g. "ClearDea · piece 2").
Sensitive Room logs the link to the matching row and piece column in the tracker, stamping the publish date.
Verify spec. Operator opens the link: in-feed short-form video, tags brand handle + @sensitiveroom.hausofk.
Verify FTC. Disclosure must be inside the post, unavoidable, and use an acceptable term (#ad / #sponsored / "[Brand] Partner"). A platform paid-partnership label alone does not pass; the creator must add their own disclosure. If FTC fails, operator flags the creator to fix before counting it (Section 10).
Flip status to live and set FTC = yes, on-time = yes/no against the +14 / +30 windows.
Notify the brand once a creator's set is complete and verified, so the brand can begin its 6-month organic repost license and shortlist the creator for paid follow-up.
Why the FTC step is strictUnder the FTC Endorsement Guides (16 CFR Part 255, revision effective July 26, 2023), there is no "organic" carve-out once a creator received something of value: a free workshop seat, gifted access, or a present-or-future paid pipeline is a material connection that must be clearly and conspicuously disclosed. Liability runs to the brand and the event organizer, not only the creator. A built-in platform label "might not be an adequate disclosure," so we require the creator to add their own. This is why the disclosure obligation sits in the creator agreement and gets verified on every single piece.
6 · Brand deal pipeline & follow-up enforcement

The free content is fair because the brand commits back

The organic pieces are not a giveaway. They are the front half of a trade: the brand gets 150 pieces of UGC at a fraction of market, and in return the brand commits to actually hire from the cohort. That commitment is what makes the free content fair to the creators.

  • The minimum. Each sponsoring brand commits to pursue at least 2 paid/branded deals from the cohort within 60 days of the workshop, sourced and booked through Sensitive Room.
  • The deal window. Any deal between a sponsoring brand and a cohort creator within 12 months of the workshop runs through Sensitive Room. This covers the first deal and all repeat, renewal, and expansion business with the same brand.
  • If a brand ghosts. If a sponsoring brand fails to extend good-faith offers meeting the minimum inside its 60-day window, the affected creators may revoke that brand's usage license to their organic content, and that content may be removed at the creator's choice. This revocation right is the linkage that keeps the trade honest.
Tracker linkageThe "Brand follow-up" column is how we enforce this. On day 60, any brand with fewer than 2 offers logged is in breach, and Sensitive Room notifies the affected creators of their revocation right. We track follow-up per brand, not just per creator.
7 · Commission & payout flow

One window collects, two buckets split

Sensitive Room is the single contracting and collecting window. It books the deal, invoices the brand, collects the cash, takes commission, and remits the partner share.

BucketWhat it isSplit (SOA : Nurilounge)
Bucket 1Sponsorship fees: the brand chapter fees ($3,000–$4,000 per brand per chapter).50 : 50
Bucket 2Branded-deal commission: the 25% of branded-deal gross value on cohort deals booked through Sensitive Room.60 : 40, SOA-weighted PROPOSED
Bucket 2 split is a proposed ask, not agreedThe 60 : 40 SOA-weighted split on branded-deal commission is a proposed negotiation ask to present to Baek Aram, justified by SOA carrying the ongoing roster management and deal brokering. It is not yet agreed. Bucket 1 at 50 : 50 is the clean, shared base. Mark Bucket 2 as proposed in any doc Aram sees until it is settled in the room.

Commission mechanics

  • Rate: 25% of branded-deal gross value. This is the market-standard single-program anchor for an emerging-creator program (the live band is 15 to 30%; 25% sits squarely in it).
  • Collection: brand pays Sensitive Room. Sensitive Room takes the 25%, then remits the creator's share, then splits its commission per Bucket 2.
  • Cadence: reconcile monthly. Send a statement showing gross, deductions, net, and amounts owed before each payout. Remit partner share within 15 days of cleared funds.
8 · Value-of-sponsorship model

Why $3K to $4K is the conservative number

The value case is volume of authentic content. Worked at the $3,500 midpoint, against 50 creators × 3 pieces = 150 organic pieces total, spread across N balanced sponsors:

SponsorsPieces per brandCost per piece at $3,500vs open-market UGC ($100–$300)
1 brand150~$23~4x to 13x cheaper
2 brands75 each~$47~2x to 6x cheaper
3 brands50 each~$70~1.4x to 4x cheaper
4 brands~38 each~$92still under market floor

Even at four sponsors, the sponsor pays below the open-market floor per piece. And the fee buys more than the pieces. On top of the content, each sponsor receives:

  • Event coverage and a reusable photo library.
  • A 2-minute brand-introduction slot in the workshop.
  • A vetted creator shortlist.
  • A 12-month deal pipeline booked through Sensitive Room.
How to pitch itLead with the cost-per-piece line for the actual sponsor count, then stack the four extras. The $3,000–$4,000 fee is deliberately conservative because Chapter 01 is a reference-builder; once the case study exists, introduce sponsor tiers and category exclusivity to lift the number.

10 · Risk & edge cases

What goes wrong, and the handling

CaseHandling
Creator no-show (attends, then never posts)Tracker flags the missed +14 / +30 windows. Sensitive Room follows up once, then once more. Non-delivery is a breach of the acceptance condition: the creator forfeits paid-shortlist consideration and is not invited to the next chapter. Their event-footage release still stands.
Brand ghosts on follow-up (under 2 offers by day 60)Brand is in breach of the follow-up minimum. Sensitive Room notifies affected creators of their right to revoke that brand's usage license; content may be removed at the creator's choice. Brand is not offered a renewal chapter until cured.
Participant under 18The name/likeness release and event-footage license require a parent or guardian to sign. Flag at acceptance; do not film or publish a minor's content under the standard adult release. Counsel should confirm child-directed and COPPA considerations before any minor is accepted.
Content-removal requestCreator retains ownership and may request removal of their own pieces. Brand's repost license is 6 months from publication and is the only thing the brand may rely on; a valid revocation (brand breach) or a minor-related issue ends the license early. Log the request and the date the brand was notified to take down reposts.
Dispute (attribution, who sourced a brand, deal credit)The tracker and the single-window booking rule are the tiebreaker: if it was not booked through Sensitive Room, it is not a program deal. Sponsorship and partnership terms are governed by the State of New York. Escalate unresolved splits to the Alex<>Aram principals.
FTC non-disclosure by a creatorVerification catches it before the piece counts. Operator sets FTC = no, does not count the piece, and tells the creator to add a compliant in-post disclosure (acceptable terms only; platform label alone does not cure). Repeated failure is a material breach of the creator agreement and removes the creator from paid consideration. This protects the brand and the organizer, who share liability under the FTC Guides.
