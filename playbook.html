6 · Deal terms at a glance

What each side signs up for

Creator
  • Free to attend.
  • Makes 3 organic pieces, mandatory to participate.
  • First piece by Jul 9 (14 days), all 3 by Jul 25 (30 days).
  • You own your content. Brand gets a 6-month organic repost license only.
  • A real shot at paid deals through the room.
Brand
  • Fee $3,000 to $4,000 per chapter.
  • Paid 100% within 7 days of the workshop. Single payment.
  • Up to 150 organic pieces across the cohort, allocated fairly.
  • 2-deal follow-up minimum within 60 days, through the room.
  • 25% commission on deals booked through Sensitive Room.
  • A 2-minute intro slot in the room.
Partner split
  • Bucket 1 · sponsorship fees: 50:50 SOA : Nurilounge.
  • Bucket 2 · commission: 60:40 SOA-weighted, proposed, see the MOU. Not yet agreed; 50:50 fallback.
  • Governing law: New York.
Keep it inside The commission percentage and the 60:40 split are fine on this internal team doc. They never appear on the public hub, the poster, the landing page, or any brand-facing or creator-facing surface. Creators see "free, make 3, shot at paid deals"; brands see "fee, content volume, 2-deal follow-up, 25% on deals we book for you."
7 · Posting plan

One handle carries the reach

Program and event posts publish from the Sensitive Ones Agency account with Nurilounge set as a collaborator (Instagram Collab / co-author), not from the Haus of K account. The point is to unify the program's posting on one handle so reach compounds, with Nuri co-credited.

  • @sensitiveroom.hausofk stays the program's DM and contact handle that creators tag.
  • Creator posts: at least one in-feed short-form video (Reel or TikTok), tag the brand handle + @sensitiveroom.hausofk + a clear #ad / #sponsored disclosure.
Pending The account-unification and collaborator-posting setup is pending Baek Aram / Nurilounge confirmation. Confirm before the first program post goes out.
8 · Tracking pointer

How we know it actually shipped

Each creator's 3 pieces are logged in Discord #content-submissions, verified against the brief, and tracked to the timeline. Full schema, submission workflow, and the verification checklist live in the Ops & Content-Tracking Playbook.

Allocation engine, one line 1 brand to 3 pieces · 3 brands to 1 each · 2 brands to a 2+1 split · 4+ brands the Room selects 3 per creator. When there is more than one brand, Sensitive Room (not the creator) picks which brands a creator covers, for fairness.

10 · Top risks for a rushed pilot

What can break, and how we catch it

RiskMitigation
Too few creators by the 25th.Recruit every day, weekend push, Daesung's network as the magnet. Pilot floor is 20; if we are short, we still run with a smaller cohort rather than slip the date.
A brand drops out.Target 3 to 4 so one loss is survivable. Get verbal commits early in the week, signatures by Mon Jun 23. The room runs even with a single brand.
Content slips past 30 days.14-day first-piece gate surfaces slippage early in the tracker; reminders at Jul 2 and Jul 16. All 3 verified in #content-submissions by Jul 25.
No-show on the night.Confirm attendance in Discord on Jun 24, send the address + time, and over-recruit against the floor to absorb drop-off.
FTC disclosure missed.The #ad / #sponsored rule is stated in the room at 0:28, written in the Participation Agreement, and checked at verification before a piece counts.
